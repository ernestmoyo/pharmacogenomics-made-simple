Gene-Drug Interaction Analysis
Warfarin — CYP2C9 (Poor Metabolizer)
CRITICAL FDA LABEL CPIC Level A
Diplotype *2/*2
Clinical Effect CYP2C9 Poor Metabolizer: Greatly reduced S-warfarin clearance; 50-80% dose reduction typically needed
Mechanism CYP2C9 is the primary enzyme for S-warfarin metabolism (the more potent enantiomer). Reduced function variants decrease warfarin clearance.
Consequence Severe bleeding; supratherapeutic INR; hemorrhage
Recommendation
Reduce starting dose to 1-2 mg/day. Use pharmacogenomic dosing algorithm incorporating both CYP2C9 and VKORC1 status. Frequent INR monitoring essential.
References Johnson JA, et al. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2017;102(3):397-404
Warfarin — VKORC1 (Moderate Sensitivity)
HIGH FDA LABEL CPIC Level A
Diplotype -1639G>A
Clinical Effect VKORC1 Moderate Sensitivity: Increased warfarin sensitivity; lower dose requirement
Mechanism VKORC1 encodes vitamin K epoxide reductase, the direct target of warfarin. Variants affect warfarin sensitivity and dose requirements.
Consequence Bleeding risk if standard doses used
Recommendation
Reduce starting dose to 3-4 mg/day. Use pharmacogenomic dosing algorithm. Monitor INR at least weekly initially.
References Johnson JA, et al. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2017;102(3):397-404

Simvastatin — SLCO1B1 (Intermediate Function)
HIGH FDA LABEL CPIC Level A
Diplotype *1a/*5
Clinical Effect SLCO1B1 Intermediate Function: Increased simvastatin exposure; elevated myopathy risk
Mechanism SLCO1B1 encodes hepatic uptake transporter OATP1B1. Reduced function increases systemic statin exposure and myopathy risk.
Consequence Myopathy risk; muscle pain; CK elevation
Recommendation
Avoid simvastatin doses >20 mg/day OR switch to rosuvastatin or pravastatin.
References Cooper-DeHoff RM, et al. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2022;111(5):1007-1021
